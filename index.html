A Visual Guide to OTC Stocks

Discover the world of Over-the-Counter (OTC) stocks, where nearly 12,400 securities trade off the major exchanges. This guide breaks down everything you need to know.

What Does "Over-the-Counter" Mean?

Instead of a centralized place like the NYSE, OTC stocks are traded directly between parties through a decentralized dealer network. Think of it as a marketplace without a single, physical trading floor.

This chart shows how the decentralized nature of OTC markets leads to key differences in regulation, transparency, and liquidity compared to highly structured major exchanges.

The Lifecycle of an OTC Stock

Companies can move between the OTC market and major exchanges. This journey highlights both the potential for success and the risks of failure.

The Golden Rule: Do Your Own Research

The lower regulatory burden on OTC companies means the responsibility falls on you, the investor, to perform thorough due diligence. Always check the market tier, review financials, and be skeptical of hype.
